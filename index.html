What You Can Do

  • Update Your Passwords

    Change passwords for any compromised accounts. Use strong, unique passwords for each account. Consider using a password manager to help you create and store secure passwords.

  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

    Add an extra layer of security by enabling 2FA on your accounts. This often involves receiving a code on your phone or email that you must enter in addition to your password.

  • Scan for Malware

    Use a reputable antivirus or anti-malware software to scan your computer or mobile device for any malicious software that might have been installed.

Prevent Future Attempts

  • Be Cautious with Unsolicited Emails and Messages

    Avoid clicking on links or download attachments from unknown or unexpected sources. Phishing often occrus via email, SMS, or social media messages.

  • Verify the Senders

    Check the email address or phone number of the sender. Be cautious if it looks suspicious or doesn't match the company's official contact information.

  • Watch for Red Flags

    Look out for generic greetings, spelling and grammar mistakes, urgent requests for personal information, and suspicious-looking links.

  • Use Official Websites

    Instead of clicking on links in emails or messages, manually type the URL of the official website into your browser.

  • Stay Informed

    Stay informed about the latest phishing tactics and scams. Knowledge is your best defense against cyber threats.
